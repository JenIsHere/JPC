About This Project

Geographic Information Systems programs at the Fleming College requires students to undertake a project for the course APST-62 GIS Collaborative Project. The team of Jenish Shah, Prashant Balachandar and Colin Chappell has taken up the project P2224 - Evaluating Urban Sprawl in the Golden Horseshoe, which was proposed by the Ontario Headwaters Institute.

About The Client

The Ontario Headwaters Institute (OHI) is a non-profit organization dedicated to the protection of Ontario's watersheds, natural heritage, and receiving rivers such as the Great Lakes. In addition to ongoing work in their three portfolios of Research, Education, and Civic Engagement.

They are now running three projects under the Projects banner above. WaterScape, Headwater Alliances, and The Water Table are a few of the organizations involved in this project.

Andrew McCammon is Executive Director at the Ontario Headwaters Institute and is the team's contact at the OHI.

Introduction

Project Statement

According to "A Place to Grow-Growth Plan", the municipalities of the Greater Golden Horseshoe (GGH) region are required to update their development plans through 2051 by the end of June 2022.

If allowed, the inreasingly imminent urban sprawl will enter the Contested Countryside lands, which would result in the loss of natural heritage and agricultural lands.

Requirements

Visualizing the Contested Countryside to shed a light on how little land is left.

Performing analyses and producing results to depict the potential impact of urban sprawl into these lands.

Creating various mediums to share the results in order to raise awareness regarding this matter.

Deliverables

A Large Format Map

A Web Map with analyses results

A Slide Deck displaying the layers
